# Accessibility

At Daily Mail, we believe that news should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their physical, sensory, or cognitive abilities. Our commitment to accessibility ensures that our global audience can engage with our "Seriously Popular" content without barriers, adhering to the **WCAG 2.1 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)** standards.

---

## 1. Our Standards

We aim for AA Level compliance across all our digital platforms, including Desktop, Mobile Web, and our iOS/Android applications.

**"Our Accessibility Commitment (AA Level)"**

- **Colour Contrast:** We ensure a minimum contrast ratio of **4.5:1** for body text and **3:1** for large headings to support users with low vision.
- **Interactive Focus:** All interactive elements must have a highly visible focus state to assist keyboard-only users.
- **Text Resizing:** Our layouts are designed to be functional even when text is scaled up to **200%** by the user's browser settings.
- **Semantic Structure:** We use consistent heading hierarchies (H1 to H6) to provide a clear logical structure for assistive technologies.

---

## 2. Core Accessibility Pillars

### 2-A. Visual Clarity & Contrast

- **Colour Contrast:** We maintain a high contrast ratio for all text and interactive elements to ensure legibility for users with visual impairments.
- **Non-Colour Indicators:** We do not rely on colour alone to convey meaning (e.g., error states or links), ensuring information is perceivable by colour-blind users.

### 2-B. Adaptive Typography

- **Scalability:** All text must be resizable up to 200% without loss of content or functionality.
- **Readability:** We prioritise clear, open-spaced typography that supports readers with dyslexia or low vision.

### 2-C. Assistive Technology Support

- **Screen Readers:** All UI components are built with proper ARIA labels and semantic HTML to provide a reliable experience for screen reader users (e.g., VoiceOver, TalkBack).
- **Keyboard Navigation:** Our web platforms are fully navigable via keyboard, ensuring clear focus states for all interactive elements.

### 2-D. Inclusive Media

- **Alt Text:** Every image conveying information must include descriptive alternative text.
- **Captions & Transcripts:** We strive to provide captions for video content to ensure our stories are inclusive of the d/Deaf and hard-of-hearing community.
